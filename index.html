Presidents

The Presidents of the United States are the nation's highest elected officials, serving as both head of state and commander-in-chief of the armed forces. They are responsible for enforcing laws, shaping national policy, and representing the country on the global stage. From George Washington to the current president, each has influenced the nation's history, politics, and society through their leadership, decisions, and vision for the country.

Did You Know?

George Washington was the only president unanimously elected.
